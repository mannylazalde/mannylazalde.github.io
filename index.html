Optimized Robotic Gait

For my final project in my Optimization class at Carnegie Mellon University during Fall 2020, my team of 4 performed trajectory optimization in MATLAB for a two legged walking robot. This involves minimizing the energy consumed for the robot to take a single step, optimizing for the joint angles, joint velocities, and joint torques over time. It was an excellent introduction into the world of optimization.

Shark Skin Biomimicry Research

I performed research in the Gu Lab at UC Berkeley throughout 2018 and 2019. We explored adapting the denticles that make up shark skin for use in modern engineering applications to increase aerodynamic performance. We have performed analysis through the use of computation fluid dynamics (CFD) in ANSYS Fluent, as well as design studies in SolidWorks. We published our work in the Bioinspiration & Biomemetics journal, feel free to read it! “Algorithmic-driven design of shark denticle bioinspired structures for superior aerodynamic properties”

CalSol Caliper Mount Redesign

Problem Statement

After noticing deflection during braking in the car's caliper mounts, my team and I had the opportunity to redesign the existing design angled bracket (caliper mount) as pictured.

Redesign Process

During the redesign process, we utilized SolidWorks FEA to determine an efficient design to minimize deflection and mass in the part. We participated in design reviews with older members to get feedback on the design.

Manufacturing

After the design process, I had the opportunity to use the UC Berkeley Machine Shop to manufacture the caliper mounts. I started with a water jet operation to cut out the general shape, then used a vertical band saw as a rough first pass to eliminate material at bulk. Finally, I utilized a mill to get the final percise dimensions.
